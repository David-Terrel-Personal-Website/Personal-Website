MMA

I have been around MMA since I was a baby. I grew up watching the best fighters of all different styles fighting and watching how different styles matched up. I learned to love the sport early on growing up in gyms seeing the fighters that I would watch fight on TV.

As I got older I started to learn the skills of a fighter and developed my wrestling as a early teen. Then I developed my boxing skills in my late teens and early 20s, and started trainging with a amatuer MMA team.

At 22 I focused purely on jiu jitsu for a year and won a few medals at tournaments in the blue belt division, and earned my purple belt. I am now 23 still focusing on Jiu jitsu, and plan on joining the pro team in my city at one of the best gyms in the world.

There is a lot more on my journey in MMA, I have enjoyed how much ive learned and look forward to what i will learn and accomplish.

Brazillian Jiu Jitsu

My first sport ever was brazillian jiu jitsu, I competed on and off throughout my childhood. Id say most of my discipline comes from my years of grappling as a kid. Jiu jitsu is a physical sport but also in my opinion the most technical.

I would compare jiu jitsu to chess. I would say Jiu jitsu is actually even more technical than chess, because there is more factors that come into play, like body type,grappling style,strength,cardio, and most importnt skill.

Doing jiu jitsu as a kid helped me transition to and excell at wrestling in highschool in my early teens .

Both of those sports together has made my grappling high level. I have just started tapping out black belts in the past year. My goal is to win a world championship in jiu jitsu.
